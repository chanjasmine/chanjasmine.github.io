The Master's Manuscript

The Master's novel about Pontius Pilate represents the search for historical and artistic truth, becoming a central element in Bulgakov's exploration of the power of art and the responsibility of the artist.

The Novel Within the Novel

The Master's manuscript tells the story of:

  • Pontius Pilate's encounter with Yeshua Ha-Nozri
  • The moral struggle of judgment and conscience
  • The weight of historical truth
  • The consequences of cowardice

The Master's Journey

The Master's personal story involves:

  • His dedication to historical truth
  • Persecution by Soviet literary critics
  • His retreat from society
  • His redemption through Margarita's love

Themes and Significance

The manuscript represents:

  • The power of art to reveal truth
  • The artist's struggle in Soviet society
  • The relationship between historical and artistic truth
  • The role of conscience in human decisions
